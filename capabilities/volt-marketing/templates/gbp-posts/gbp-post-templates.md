### Template 14: Post-Storm Electrical Inspection
**Category:** Storm Response
**When to use:** 2–5 days after a major storm event when immediate emergencies have subsided
**Post text:**
The storm has passed, the power is back on, and everything seems fine. But is it?

Electrical storm damage doesn't always announce itself. Lightning can degrade surge protectors silently. Wind can loosen connections at your weatherhead without visible signs. Hail can crack conduit and outdoor fixture housings, letting moisture in where it doesn't belong.

Here's what we recommend after any major storm in Colorado Springs:

**Check these yourself:**
- Test all GFCI outlets (kitchen, bathroom, garage, outdoor) with the test/reset buttons
- Look at your panel — any tripped breakers that weren't tripped before?
- Walk your exterior and look at the service mast, weatherhead, and any outdoor wiring
- Check your surge protector indicators — many have a light that goes out when protection is spent

**Schedule a professional inspection if:**
- You experienced a direct or nearby lightning strike
- Any breakers tripped during the storm
- You notice new flickering, buzzing, or warm outlets
- Your home is older or hasn't been inspected in several years

Post-storm inspections are quick, affordable, and can catch problems before they become expensive — or dangerous.
