# Storm Response — High Wind
**Template ID:** storm-response-wind
**Category:** Storm Response
**Trigger:** `high-wind.yaml`
**When to use:** During or after a high wind event (sustained 40+ mph or gusts 58+ mph)

---

Those winds were no joke. When gusts hit 60+ mph in Colorado Springs, electrical damage follows — even if you don't see it.

High winds cause electrical problems in ways most homeowners don't think about:
- **Service mast strain** — your mast can shift or loosen where it enters your home, breaking the weather seal
- **Tree limb contact** — branches rubbing or falling on your service drop (the line from the pole to your house) can damage insulation
- **Antenna and satellite movement** — rooftop equipment can shift and stress the wiring below
- **Debris impact** — flying debris can damage outdoor panels, disconnects, and conduit

**Check these after a high wind event:**
- Walk your exterior and look up at the service mast — is it straight? Any visible gaps where it enters the house?
- Check for branches on or near overhead service lines (do NOT touch — call your utility first)
- Look at outdoor panels, disconnects, and junction boxes for damage or displaced covers
- Test your GFCI outlets — wind-driven rain can trip exterior circuits

**Call Phoenix Electric if:**
- Your service mast looks tilted, bent, or pulled away from the house
- You hear buzzing from your panel or outdoor equipment
- Breakers are tripping after the wind event
- You can see damaged or exposed wiring anywhere on your exterior
