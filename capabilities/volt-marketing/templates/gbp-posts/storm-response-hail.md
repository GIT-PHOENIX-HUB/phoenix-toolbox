Colorado Springs just took another round of hail, and your roof isn't the only thing that may have been damaged.

Hail — especially the golf-ball-size storms we get along the Front Range — can crack electrical conduit, shatter outdoor light fixtures, dent weatherheads, and damage disconnect boxes and panel enclosures. That damage may not cause an immediate failure, but it opens the door to moisture intrusion, corrosion, and eventual shorts.

**After a hail event, check for:**
- Cracked or dented conduit runs along your exterior walls
- Broken outdoor light fixtures or landscape lighting
- Visible dents or damage to your electrical mast or weatherhead
- Water stains or moisture near your panel (especially if it's on an exterior wall)
- Your surge protector indicator light — a nearby lightning strike can deplete your protection

**Call us if:**
- Breakers are tripping that weren't tripping before the storm
- You smell burning or notice warm outlets/switches
- Your generator is showing error codes after running during the outage
- Any outdoor electrical components show visible hail damage

Most homeowner insurance policies cover hail damage to electrical systems — we can document findings for your claim.
