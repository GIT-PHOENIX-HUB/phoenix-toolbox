# Storm Response — Winter Storm

**When to use:** During or after a winter storm (heavy snow, ice storm, blizzard, freeze event)

---

Winter storms in Colorado Springs bring more than snow days — ice, heavy snow loads, and freezing temperatures can cause real electrical damage.

**Ice is the biggest risk.** Ice accumulation on power lines, tree branches, and your service drop is the #1 cause of winter electrical outages. When ice-loaded branches snap onto lines, the damage cascades — and your home's electrical system takes the hit.

**After a winter storm, watch for:**
- **Generator issues** — if your Generac ran during the outage, check for error codes and confirm it returned to "auto" standby. Extended cold-weather runs stress the system.
- **Frozen or burst conduit** — outdoor conduit can crack when water inside freezes and expands. Look for splits or bulging.
- **Ice dam moisture** — ice dams on your roof can force water into wall cavities where wiring runs. Watch for tripping breakers in upper-floor circuits.
- **Heater overload** — space heaters on shared circuits are the #1 cause of winter breaker trips. If a circuit keeps tripping, you may need a dedicated circuit.

**Call Phoenix Electric if:**
- Your generator failed to start or showed errors during the outage
- You see ice-damaged service lines or mast
- Breakers are tripping on heating circuits
- You experienced a power surge when power was restored (flickering, popping, equipment damage)
- You want a whole-home surge protector installed before the next storm
