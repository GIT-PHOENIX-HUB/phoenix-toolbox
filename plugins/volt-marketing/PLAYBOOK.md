## How to Talk to Volt

Volt responds best to specific, actionable questions. The more context you give, the more precise the output.

### Great Questions to Ask Volt

**Campaign Planning:**
- "Build me a Q2 Google Ads campaign for Generac generators targeting Castle Rock and Elizabeth"
- "Create a Facebook ad campaign for kitchen remodels in Highlands Ranch, budget $1,500/month"
- "What's the best marketing mix for $3,000/month focused on service calls and generators?"

**Ad Copy:**
- "Write 5 Google Search ad headlines for emergency electrical service in Parker"
- "Create a Nextdoor post announcing our Generac spring promotion"
- "Draft a direct mail postcard for homes over 20 years old in Centennial"

**Seasonal Strategy:**
- "What should our marketing focus be this month?"
- "Build me a storm season activation playbook for Generac"
- "Plan our pre-winter generator marketing push for September through November"

**Budget & ROI:**
- "How should I allocate $2,500/month across channels?"
- "What's the expected CPL for Google LSA vs PPC for service calls?"
- "If I spend $1,000/month on Facebook, how many leads should I expect?"

**Competitive Intelligence:**
- "How do we dominate local search in Elizabeth and Elbert County?"
- "What's our competitive advantage as a Generac authorized dealer?"
- "How many Google reviews do we need to rank in the top 3 for Parker?"

## Playbook 1: Storm Season Generac Blitz (March-June)

This is your highest-ROI marketing window. Colorado spring storms drive generator demand.

### Ask Volt:
```
"Create a complete storm season Generac marketing plan for March through June.
Include Google Ads with storm-chasing activation protocol, Facebook awareness
campaign, direct mail to rural Elbert County, and a Generac co-op funded component.
Budget tier: Growth ($2,500/month)."
```

### What Volt Will Deliver:
- Google PPC campaign with storm-activated keywords and budget surge rules
- Facebook awareness campaign targeting homeowners 45-70, $150K+ income, in outage-prone areas
- Direct mail sequence for rural Elizabeth/Elbert County properties
- Generac co-op marketing fund application guidance
- Storm-chasing protocol: ad copy pre-written, ready to activate within hours of outage
- Tracking setup with dedicated landing page and call tracking number

## Pro Tips

1. **Stack your playbooks.** Run the service call pipeline AND the Generac blitz simultaneously — they feed each other.
2. **Always ask for negative keywords** when Volt recommends Google Ads. This prevents wasted spend outside your territory.
3. **Request 3 budget tiers** so you can start at Starter and scale to Growth when you see results.
4. **Use the storm protocol before storm season** — have everything pre-built and ready to activate, not scrambling after the fact.
5. **Feed Volt your actual numbers** for quarterly reviews — the more real data, the better the optimization recommendations.
6. **Cross-reference with Electrical Guru** — when Volt recommends promoting a service, use the electrical-guru skill to ensure your technical claims are code-accurate.
